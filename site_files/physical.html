Clean Desk Tips

  • Don’t leave hard copies containing private information on your desk. It’s easy for visitors or others to see protected data that is left in plain sight.
  • Securely store protected data that’s in hard copy form in a secured drawer or cabinet.
  • Dispose of protected data in hard copy form in compliance with Firm policies, for example, shredding documents or disposing of them in designated receptacles.
  • Don’t make unnecessary copies of documents that contain protected data or distribute such documents beyond those who need the documents.
  • When printing documents that contain protected data, be sure to pick up the copies in a timely manner to minimize the possibility of them being inadvertently misplaced or picked up by someone else.

Physical Security

A law firm’s reputation is an integral part of its success. When we think of data breaches, we usually think about hackers or malware attacks as the cause; however, studies show that almost as many data breaches are the result of carelessness. Some of the ways we can inadvertently cause a data breach include mishandling client records, information theft, and disregarding internal security protocols. As a security protection group recently affirmed, security awareness is about PREVENTING problems, not responding to them.

Precautions should be taken to secure the front desk, conference rooms, your own office space, and the firm’s physical files. Being aware of physical security concerns, ensure that firm’s reputation for the protection and use of client files is of the highest caliber.

Protect Paper-based Information

Sensitive information requires diligence regardless of the media on which it is stored. Confidential information is treated with some level of sensitivity, but there may not be legal consequences to unauthorized access or disclosure of it. Protected information is highly sensitive information protected by law or regulation and requires the highest level of access control and protection while in storage or transit. Unlike confidential information, protected information has defined legal consequences to not securing the information appropriately.
