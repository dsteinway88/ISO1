Message # 1

Focus: Confidential and Protected Information

Sender: IT Department or Risk Management Representative

The idea of confidential information isn’t new to law firms, and most firms have policies in place that address the need for keeping certain things confidential, such as the identity of firm clients, settlement information, or even conflict check information which identifies potential clients. However, confidential information is not the same as protected information.

Confidential information is a broad term which, although it does imply some level of sensitivity and should be appropriately secured, it is not the term we will use to identify information which is specifically addressed by law or regulation.

Protected information is the term we use for highly sensitive information that is protected by law or regulation. It requires the highest level of access control and security protection while in storage or in transit. Unlike confidential information, with protected information there are defined legal consequences to not securing the information appropriately.

Due to the diverse nature of our work, many types of protected information are stored at the Firm, and must be handled with extreme care to ensure we do not inadvertently disclose the information. Examples of protected data include:

·         Personally Identifying Information (PII)

·         Protected Health Information (PHI) or Electronic Protected Health Information (ePHI)

·         Payment Card Industry data (PCI), such as credit card information

One of the key characteristics of protected information is that there is often a requirement to notify certain agencies in the event of a data breach in which protected information is inadvertently disclosed.

So, in the spirit of the great philosophers: all protected information is confidential; not all confidential information is protected.

To learn more about protected information, how to safeguard it, and who to contact if you have questions, please visit our Security Awareness site.
