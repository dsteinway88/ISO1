Responsible Mobile Device Use

Under the ABA Model Rules of Professional Conduct, lawyers are required to make reasonable efforts to protect clients' confidential information from inadvertent or unauthorized disclosures or unauthorized access by third parties. In keeping with this directive, the Firm has an ethical obligation to protect client data that is stored on mobile devices.

There are a number of best practices related to mobile device use which can limit risk and the possibility of exposing client or Firm data:

  • First, familiarize yourself with the Firm’s policies regarding the use of mobile devices.
  • Use strong passwords to secure your device and always use secure wireless connections when working on your mobile device.
  • Additionally, ensure that the operating software on your device is current, as many updates include patches that specifically address security vulnerabilities.
  • Lastly, if your device is lost or stolen, report it immediately by submitting a Help Desk Ticket, or contact Information Security directly.
