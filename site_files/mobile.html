Responsible Mobile Device Use

All firm employees are required to make reasonable efforts to protect clients' confidential information from inadvertent or unauthorized disclosures or unauthorized access by third parties. In keeping with this directive, the Firm has an obligation to protect client data that is stored on mobile devices.

Approved Devices

The following mobile devices are generally approved for use by Attorney and Administrative Staff.

  • Firm-provided laptops & tablets
  • Personal and firm-issued smartphones
  • Approved USB storage devices

Firm employees may be required to qualify for certain devices based on job role. Please consult the Mobile Communications & Remote Access Policy for more information.

Best Practices

There are a number of best practices related to mobile device use which can limit risk and the possibility of exposing client or Firm data:

  • First, familiarize yourself with the Firm’s policies regarding the use of mobile devices.
  • Use strong passwords to secure your device and always use secure wireless connections when working on your mobile device.
  • Additionally, ensure that the operating software on your device is current, as many updates include patches that specifically address security vulnerabilities.
  • Lastly, if your device is lost or stolen, report it immediately by submitting a Help Desk Ticket, or contact Information Security directly.
